# Whiteboard Context

Source: user-provided whiteboard photo, 2026-04-27.

## Interpreted Product Shape

Reeva is drawn as the central automation hub for corporate reimbursement. The whiteboard shows Reeva between:

- Employee/funcionario: submits reimbursement requests and receives an approval/rejection outcome.
- Manager/gestor: approves or rejects reimbursement requests, with an operational flow around review.
- ERP: sends customer/client/business data into Reeva.
- CFO/director: receives ROI visibility from the system.

The word "automatico" appears inside/near Reeva, implying that the product should reduce manual work in the reimbursement process. A crossed-out "reembolso" box on the left suggests replacing or improving a legacy/manual reimbursement process.

## Product Implications

- Reeva should be positioned as workflow automation, not only OCR.
- OCR is one automation capability inside a broader reimbursement engine.
- The approval workflow needs explicit yes/no outcomes and clear manager accountability.
- ERP integration matters for customer/client data, cost centers, departments, and financial context.
- CFO/director users need ROI, savings, cycle time, exception volume, and operational visibility.
- The product should expose operational status: submitted, automatically analyzed, approved, rejected, revision needed, finance/payment state.

## Planning Consequences

- Phase 1 remains AI OCR Reliability, but decisions should optimize for end-to-end reimbursement automation.
- Future roadmap should include ERP ingestion, executive ROI dashboard, and operational review metrics.
- Data model changes should preserve auditability across employee, manager, finance, and executive reporting.
